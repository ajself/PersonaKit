# Persona Hiring Research Loop

Use this essential when reverse-interview confidence is low or unknowns block a reliable hiring verdict.

## Trigger Conditions

Run this loop when any of the following is true:

1. Assessor states: "I do not know enough about this role/domain."
2. Confidence score is below the required threshold.
3. High-severity gaps are based on assumption rather than evidence.
4. Key role requirements cannot be mapped to candidate artifacts.

## Research Loop

1. Define the knowledge gap:
   - What must be known to judge candidate fit?
   - What is currently unknown?
2. Gather role evidence:
   - Collect role expectations and constraints from project context.
   - Identify must-have behaviors, deliverables, and risk controls.
3. Gather candidate evidence:
   - Persona responsibilities, values, and non-goals.
   - Linked kits, essentials, intents, directives, and sessions.
   - Relevant planning and continuity logs.
4. Compile notes:
   - Keep a concise evidence list with source artifact IDs/paths.
   - Mark each point as `confirmed`, `inferred`, or `unknown`.
5. Distill and prune:
   - Keep only signals that materially affect hiring confidence.
   - Remove redundant, weak, or speculative claims.
6. Re-evaluate fit:
   - Re-score using the hiring confidence rubric.
   - Update verdict (`qualified`, `qualified-with-gaps`, `not-yet-qualified`).
7. Decide next step:
   - If confidence is still low, propose explicit data needed.
   - If confidence is sufficient, propose prioritized artifact updates.

## Output Requirements

Every low-confidence pass must include:

1. Confidence score before and after research.
2. Unknowns list and how each unknown affected risk.
3. Evidence list with source references.
4. Revised verdict and top recommended action.

## Guardrails

- No artifact edits are allowed during research-only passes.
- Distinguish facts from inference explicitly.
- Do not claim confidence without evidence-backed signals.
- Pause for AJ review before any structural updates.
