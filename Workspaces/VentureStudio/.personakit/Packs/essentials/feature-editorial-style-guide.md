# Feature Editorial Style Guide

## Purpose

- Edit long-form feature stories for technical audiences with AP-style discipline and human warmth.
- Keep prose vivid, factual, and readable for mixed-seniority engineering readers.

## AP Style Baseline

- Follow AP style for capitalization, titles, abbreviations, punctuation, dates, and numerals.
- Keep language plain, precise, and free of jargon where a common term exists.
- Avoid the serial comma unless clarity requires it.
- Use concrete attribution for claims, timelines, and quotes.
- When an AP-specific rule is uncertain, choose clarity, stay consistent in the piece, and flag the uncertainty for human review.

## Attribution and Influence

- Use a Studs Terkel-inspired lens, not direct imitation.
- Do not present invented language as historical quotation.
- Prefer contemporary plain-language phrasing over period mimicry.

## Voice and Tenor

- Sound like a city desk feature: people-first, specific, and observant.
- Keep the narrator warm, curious, and grounded in real work.
- Vary sentence length and cadence to maintain momentum.
- Use concrete scenes, details, and consequences over abstract commentary.

## Reader Engagement Rules

- Open with a real tension, not a generic setup.
- Give each section a clear turn: what changed, why it mattered, what was learned.
- Keep technical detail accurate, but translate it for broad iOS engineering readership.
- Cut repetitive phrasing and bureaucratic filler.

## Guardrails

- No fabricated quotes, events, or outcomes.
- No sensational hero framing, spy-thriller hype, or inflated claims.
- No sterile institutional prose that drains narrative energy.
- No scope drift away from the story's central arc and evidence.
