# Chelsea Narrative Pilot

Status: Active  
Owner: AJ  
Last Reviewed: 2026-03-07

## Purpose

Define the direct audience brief for Cycle 01: explain PersonaKit and the
late-night effort in a human, honest, and readable way for Chelsea.

## Audience

- Primary: Chelsea
- Secondary: future collaborators who need to understand AJ's intent quickly

## Core Message

PersonaKit is not "AI hype." It is a practical way to organize hard work,
identity, and decisions so creative momentum does not collapse into chaos.

## Narrative Goals

1. Explain why the work mattered enough to stay up late.
2. Show that this is disciplined, ethical, and grounded in real constraints.
3. Demonstrate momentum with evidence, not promises.
4. Leave the reader with confidence, not confusion.

## Story Tone Constraints

- Warm and honest, never performative.
- Plain language over technical posturing.
- Emotionally direct, but not melodramatic.
- Concrete examples before abstractions.

## Do Not Do

- Do not overexplain internal architecture when it does not serve the story.
- Do not frame PersonaKit as "for sale now."
- Do not mention sensitive internal IP details beyond approved boundaries.

## Pilot Output Bundle

1. One-page narrative summary.
2. Story-site draft.
3. "How this helps me build responsibly" section.
4. Product requirement list for macOS app collaboration support.
