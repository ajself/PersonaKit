# Orbit Platform Positioning

Orbit is the command center for running AI teams.

Where most AI products focus on single conversations with a model, Orbit focuses on **structured collaboration between persistent AI roles** working together across projects.

Orbit enables a founder or individual engineer to operate like a small startup team — with AI collaborators taking on roles such as product manager, designer, engineer, researcher, or reviewer.

---

# Category

Orbit sits adjacent to several existing software categories:

- AI assistants
- multi-agent frameworks
- collaboration platforms

However, Orbit is not simply a better chatbot, nor a developer framework for agents.

Orbit introduces a different model:

**AI collaborators organized into persistent teams.**

In Orbit, AI systems are not treated as tools that answer prompts. They are treated as **team members with roles, memory, and history**.

---

# Core Message

Orbit lets founders and individual engineers run teams of AI collaborators across multiple ventures.

Instead of juggling prompts across chat tools, documents, and notes, users work inside a structured environment where AI collaborators participate in discussions, meetings, and decisions.

Each workspace becomes a place where:

- ideas are discussed
- decisions are recorded
- context accumulates
- teams learn over time

Orbit turns fragmented AI interactions into **coherent collaborative work.**

---

# Key Differentiators

Orbit provides capabilities that are rare or missing in existing AI tools.

### Persistent Personas

AI collaborators have stable roles and identities. They are not recreated from scratch in every prompt.

### Workspace Context

Each project workspace develops its own context, decisions, and knowledge.

### Structured Meetings

Collaboration occurs through coordinated discussions between personas rather than isolated responses.

### Durable Memory

Important knowledge is promoted through journaling and review instead of being lost in chat history.

### Human‑Directed Learning

The human founder remains the center of gravity. AI collaborators evolve through experience, but memory and decisions remain under human control.
