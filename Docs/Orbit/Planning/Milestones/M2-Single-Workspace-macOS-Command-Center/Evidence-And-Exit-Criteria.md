# M2 Evidence And Exit Criteria

Status: Draft
Milestone: `M2`
Owner: `senior-swiftui-engineer`
Last Updated: 2026-03-18

## Purpose

Define the proof package required to close `M2` honestly.

## Hero Proof

`M2` should produce one convincing checkpoint proof that includes all of these in
one reviewable sequence:

- first open into the Orbit workspace
- visible founding roster
- one attributable collaborator response
- one operator-visible trace inspection
- one restart that preserves the thread and attribution
- one lightweight multi-participant exchange that still feels understandable

If that sequence is weak, confusing, or under-evidenced, `M2` is not done.

## Required Artifacts

1. quality bar for the first Orbit room
2. command-center experience bar
3. rerun execution contract
4. golden checkpoint walkthrough
5. validation and review matrix
6. product acceptance result
7. interaction-quality review artifact
8. snapshot-based visual proof for the room surface
9. validation closeout artifact
10. participant-evidence artifact
11. red-pen evidence artifact
12. retrospective closeout artifacts
13. operator-visible proof that trace inspection is available from the room

## Exit Checklist

`M2` exits only when all of these are true:

- AJ can open the Orbit workspace and immediately understand where they are
- AJ can see the founding collaborators as durable participants
- AJ can start or continue a thread
- the thread survives restart
- speaker attribution remains intact after reload
- direct addressing works in a reviewable way
- lightweight multi-participant behavior is understandable enough to defend
- activation trace is discoverable, useful, and lightweight
- snapshot-based visual review exists for first-open, seeded or empty, and trace
  states that matter to Orbit identity
- the room feels structurally different from generic chat
- the product acceptance review passes, or passes with notes that do not weaken
  Orbit's command-center identity
- the interaction-quality review passes, or passes with notes that do not weaken
  clarity, stability, or explainability
- deterministic validation review passes
- red-pen evidence exists for all active owners
- retrospective closeout is complete

## Residual Open Dependencies

- `M2` still depends on `M1` being a trustworthy identity and activation
  baseline
- `M2` still depends on `M0` having resolved or explicitly staged the founding
  roster naming decision

Those are real dependencies and should stay visible.

## Not Enough To Exit

These do not count as success:

- an Orbit sidebar item exists
- a thread exists but durability is shaky
- trace metadata exists only in logs or debug views
- the room works technically but still feels like chat with labels
- the branch is described as `review-ready` without the full closeout packet

## Review Gate

Before `M3` is allowed to treat this checkpoint as a trustworthy local baseline,
AJ should be able to review a small, convincing packet containing:

- the golden walkthrough
- the product acceptance result
- the interaction-quality review artifact
- the validation closeout artifact
- the participant and red-pen evidence manifests
- the retrospective closeout summary
- proof that trace inspection is available from the room surface

If that packet does not feel sharp, `M2` should remain open.
