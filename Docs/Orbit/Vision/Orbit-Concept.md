# Orbit

Orbit is the command center for running AI teams.

A single founder can operate multiple ventures, projects, and research initiatives using persistent AI collaborators. Orbit provides a structured environment where those collaborators can work together across conversations, meetings, and long‑term learning.

Unlike traditional AI tools that focus on a single conversational assistant, Orbit is designed to support **collaboration between multiple specialized roles** that operate together as a team.

---

## What Orbit Provides

Orbit provides the structural elements needed to run an AI team rather than a collection of isolated prompts:

- **Workspaces** for each venture, product, or research effort
- **Teams and squads** that organize collaboration inside a workspace
- **Structured meetings** where multiple personas deliberate and contribute perspectives
- **Durable conversations** that remain inspectable and replayable over time
- **Journals and memory** that allow the system to learn responsibly
- **Coordinated AI collaboration** rather than isolated responses

These elements transform AI interaction from ad‑hoc prompting into **structured teamwork**.

Orbit is not simply a chat interface. It is a system for managing the **roles, discussions, and knowledge** that emerge when AI collaborators work together over time.

---

## Core Idea

Orbit is built around a simple principle:

> The human is the center of gravity.

AI collaborators do not replace the founder. They orbit around the founder and assist with thinking, planning, design, engineering, research, and review.

Personas act as specialized collaborators that:

- provide perspectives from different professional roles
- debate ideas during structured discussions
- record decisions and lessons learned
- accumulate expertise through memory and journaling

The system is designed to help a single founder operate with the leverage of a small, coordinated team.

---

## Key Concepts

Orbit introduces several concepts that organize how collaboration happens.

### Workspace

A workspace represents a venture, product, or research initiative.

Each workspace acts as a boundary for:

- conversations
- teams and squads
- workspace personas
- journals
- memory

This prevents knowledge from one project from accidentally contaminating another.

### Team

A team is a durable group of personas responsible for a broad area of work.

Examples:

- Product Team
- Engineering Team
- Research Team

Teams provide organizational continuity within a workspace.

### Squad

A squad is a focused working group formed around a specific initiative.

Examples:

- Onboarding Squad
- Growth Experiments Squad
- Architecture Review Squad

Squads allow smaller groups to collaborate without involving every persona in every discussion.

### Persona

A persona represents a persistent role that participates in discussions and meetings.

Examples include:

- Product Manager
- Product Designer
- Senior Engineer
- Researcher

Personas are not ephemeral prompts. They maintain identity, context, and evolving expertise within a workspace.

---

## Orbit and PersonaKit

Orbit is the **platform** where collaboration occurs.

PersonaKit is the **engine** inside Orbit that powers persona identity and reasoning.

PersonaKit provides:

- persona templates
- workspace persona instances
- persona activation
- directives that guide reasoning
- grounding and memory retrieval

Orbit provides the environment where those personas collaborate across workspaces, meetings, and decisions.

This separation allows the platform and reasoning engine to evolve independently while remaining tightly integrated.

---

## Conceptual Model

The relationship between the founder, Orbit, and personas can be visualized as:

```
Founder
   ↓
Orbit Platform
   ↓
Workspaces
   ↓
Teams / Squads
   ↓
Personas
```

Each layer provides additional structure that allows collaboration to scale from a single interaction to a coordinated system of AI collaborators working across many projects.

---

## Why Orbit Exists

Most AI tools focus on conversations with a single model.

Orbit focuses on **collaboration between roles**.

Instead of asking a single assistant for answers, the founder can interact with a team that includes perspectives such as:

- product manager
- designer
- engineer
- researcher
- reviewer

Each role contributes a different perspective to a problem.

Over time, these collaborators develop a deeper understanding of the founder’s preferences, the project’s history, and the decisions that shaped the product.

Orbit therefore transforms AI from a tool you prompt into a **team you work with**.
